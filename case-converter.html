Case Types Explained

UPPERCASE

All letters are capitalized. Commonly used for headings, emphasis, and acronyms.

HELLO WORLD

lowercase

All letters are in lowercase. Often used for casual text and email addresses.

hello world

Title Case

First letter of each word is capitalized. Used for titles, headlines, and proper names.

Hello World

Sentence case

Only first letter of sentences is capitalized. Standard for regular text and paragraphs.

Hello world

camelCase

First word lowercase, subsequent words capitalized. Common in programming.

helloWorld

snake_case

Words separated by underscores. Popular in Python and other programming languages.

hello_world

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Title Case and Sentence case?

Title Case capitalizes the first letter of every word, while Sentence case only capitalizes the first letter of sentences and proper nouns.

When should I use camelCase vs snake_case?

camelCase is commonly used in JavaScript, Java, and C#, while snake_case is preferred in Python, Ruby, and some other languages. It depends on the programming language conventions.

Can I convert multiple paragraphs at once?

Yes! Our case converter works with any length of text, including multiple paragraphs, lists, and complex documents.

Does the converter preserve formatting like line breaks?

Yes, the converter preserves line breaks and paragraph structure while changing only the letter case.
